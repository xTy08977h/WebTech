TIPS TO MASTERING PISTOL SQUATS
A full pistol squat in which one leg is forward and parallel to the floor is great for advanced exercisers who have mastered the stability and balance elements. A modified version done on a bench or plyo box will challenge you while you gain all the benefits of the full move. This move can work your strength, balance, and flexibility Opens a New Window. Any unilateral move, working one side or one leg at a time, helps you reach your full strength potential, notes Megan Dahlman, C.S.C.S., owner of Dahlman Elite Training Systems in Aurora, OR. Pistol squats Opens a New Window. challenge the quads, but they also work the glutes, inner and outer thighs, and even the small ankle joint muscles; a strong core helps you put it all together, adds Dahlman.

TIPS FOR BIGGER, BETTER SQUATS
First of all, do your normal working sets of squats. Secondly, follow video doing 3 sets of 6 reps of narrow, or bodybuilder squats. Next, perform 3 sets of 6 repetitions of front squats, another quad dominant exercise. After your front squats, do 3 sets of 5 repetitions of wide squats. After you finish your wide stance squats, you are going to do 3 sets of 5 reps of Kang squats, a posterior chain movement. After you have finished all these squat variations, do 3 sets of 10 reps of glute ham raises.
